Brands

Gibson

Gibson, founded in 1952 in Kalamazoo, Michigan by Orville Gibson, is an American music instruments company. Across it's 115 year history, Gibson has a consistent track record of innovation. The founder, Orville Gibson, invented the ""archtop"" style of jazz guitar. In the 1930s, Gibson released the ES 150, the first commercially successful hollowbody electric guitar. In 1952, Gibson collaborated with jazz guitarist and inventor Les Paul to release the Gibson Les Paul, one of the most iconic electric guitars of all time.

Fender

Fender Musical Instruments Corporation, founded in 1946 in Fullertown, California by Clarence ""Leo"" Fender, is an American music instruments company. The Fender Telecaster, released in 1950, was the first commercially successful solidbody electric guitar. The Fender Stratocaster, released in 1954, is still of the most iconic, and widely used guitars of all time, known for its versatility and durability.

Gretsch

Gretsch, founded in 1883 by Friedrich Gretsch in Ridgeland, South Carolina, is an American music instruments company. In the 1950s and 1960s, Gretsch hollowbody electric guitars were used by many rock'n'roll and rockabilly guitarists, such as Chuck Berry, John Lennon, and Eddie Cochran. They were known for their distinct twangy sound. Unfortunately, in the 70s they fell out of favor.

PRS

Paul Reed Smith Guitars is an American guitar company founded in Stephensville, Maryland by Paul Reed Smith, in 1985. Smith started as an independent guitar builder in college. The guitars he designed for Carlos Santana got him national attention. Today, PRS is known for its high end electricity guitars, that often draw inspiration from other models of guitars, such as Fender and Guitar.
