Guitarists

Jimi Hendrix

Jimi Hendrix was an American guitarist. Born in Seattle in 1942, Hendrix is widely considered to be the greatest electric guitarist of all time. He got his start playing as a backing guitarist in R&B bands, before developing his own style, combining hard rock, jazz, blues, and experimental music

Jimmy Page

Jimmy Page is an English guitarist, songwriter and producer. Born in 1944, he is best known as the guitarist, and founder of the legendary rock band Led Zeppelin. Page started off his career in the early 1960s as a session musician. In 1965, he joined the Yardbirds. After a string of departures, Jimmy Page was the only member left. He found replacements, but decided to rename the group Led Zeppelin, and head in a heavier, more experimental direction.

Mark Knopfler

Mark Knopfler is a Brittish guitarist, singer, and songwriter. Born in 1949, he is best known as the guitarist, and one of the founding members of the band Dire Straits. Before achieving fame, Knopfler worked as an English teacher for some time. After the break-up of Dire Straits, Knopfler has ventured into country music, and composed film scores, most notably The Princess Bride.

Kurt Cobain

Kurt Cobain was an American guitarist, singer, and songwriter. Born in 1967, he formed the band Nirvana in Seattle 1987. Nirvana was the first band in the Seattle grunge"" scence to achieve widespread mainstream success. Unfortunately he struggled with fame, drug addiction, and mental illness. He committed suicide in 1994.
